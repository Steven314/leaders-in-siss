Introduction

“These -isms (and especially post-modernism) I regard as having set the discipline back, and to have severely detracted from the standing that human geography has gained as an analytical science as a result of the ‘quantitative revolution’.”

– Robert J. Stimson, 2007

Who

Robert John Stimson

Emeritus Professor
School of the Environment
The University of Queensland, Australia

“As a second year high school student as 13, I was taught geography… from that early age I was ‘hooked’ on geography.”

What

What areas did Stimson study?

Human Geography

“During the 1970s I became more convinced that human geographers needed to work in multi-displinary teams and use rigorous quantitative methods to address challenging socio-spatial issues to develop a better ‘evidence base’ to inform public policy.”

  • Healthcare Access
  • Survey Methods & Data Collection
  • Regional Development and Foreign Investment
  • Housing Policy
  • Quality of Life

Where

  • Early Life: Armidale, New South Wales
  • Early Career: Adelaide, South Australia
    • Stimson also spent some time at the University of Michigan.
  • Middle Career: Canberra, Australian Capital Territory
  • Later Career: Brisbane, Queensland

When

  • 1944 - Born
  • 1960 - Began undergraduate at age 16.
  • 1963 - Started teaching high school geography and economics.
  • 1965 - Began tutoring at the University of Melbourne.
  • 1968 - Began as a lecturer at Flinders University.
  • 1976 - Visited the University of Michigan.
  • 1978 - Finished his Ph.D.
  • early 1980s - Took a leadership position at University of Canberra.
  • 1997 - Joined the University of Queensland.
  • 2000s - Various leadership positions at Queensland and Melbourne.
  • 2010s to Present - Still active in the field.

Some dates are inferred based on the context.

Education

1960 - 1978

Educational Background

  • Bachelor of Arts: University of New England, 1960 - 1963
    • Focusing on geography, geology, economics, and history.
  • Bachelor of Letters: University of New England, mid-1960s
    • Further focusing on geography.
  • Doctor of Philosophy: Flinders University of South Australia, ~1970 - 1978
    • Social Space, Preference Space and Residential Location Behaviour: A Social Geography of Adelaide1

  1. I would have liked to find a copy of this, but was unable to do so.

Influences

  • Stimson’s father was involved in academic administration at the University of New England.
  • He was taught geography in high school by Mal Logan, a prominent geographer in Australia, leading to his interest in the field.
  • Stimson’s education was concurrent with the quantitative revolution in geography.
  • He was a heavy reader of various prominent figures.
    • Johann Heinrich von Thünen - land use and rent, marginal cost
    • Walter Christaller - Central Place Theory
    • Peter Haggett - quantitative human geography
    • Richard Chorley - Systems Theory, physical geography

Early Career

1968 - 1983, The ‘Sales and Service’ Era

Academic Work

  • Stimson was involved with attracting research dollars from government and industry.
  • The topics of study were
    • access to healthcare and
    • regional impacts of new healthcare facilities.
  • University of Michigan, Institute for Social Research
    • Stimson spent time developing survey methods.
    • This resulted in the formation of Centre for Applied Social and Survey Research (CASSR) at Flinders.
      • Stimson was its director for seven years.
      • CASSR surveyed healthcare, housing, entertainment, and voting behavior.

Multi-Displinary

  • Spatially Integrated Social Science is multi-disciplinary.
  • Stimson recognized the need for combining distinct fields.

“During the 1970s I became more convinced that human geographers needed to work in multi-displinary teams and use rigorous quantitative methods to address challenging socio-spatial issues to develop a better ‘evidence base’ to inform public policy.”

“One of the encouraging things that happened at this time was our ability to form small multi-displinary research teams…”

Regional Science Association International

  • In reaction to the -isms of the 1970s, Stimson became associated with the Regional Science Association International.
  • He helped form the Australia and New Zealand section and is recognized as a “Life Member” of ANZRSAI.
  • Stimson was president of the section from 1980 to 1982.
  • He was president of RSAI in 2005 and 2006.

Middle Career

1983 - 1997

Shift to Leadership Roles

Australian Institue of Urban Studies (AIUS), 1983

  • Stimson left Flinders to go to the University of Canberra1, becoming Director of AIUS.
  • He began to focus more on the public and business concerns of policy related to regional development.

Head of the School of Administrative Studies, 1985

  • Stimson interacted with federal-level public policy and administration.
  • He saw that there was value in spatial thinking and analysis.
  • In addition to adminstrative duties, Stimson researched the effects of policy on housing and published a book.

  1. At the time it was called Canberra College of Advanced Education.

The Brisbane Plan, 1990

  • The goal was a 20-year plan for developing the city of Brisbane.
  • The project was more oriented more toward policy and planning, so it was a shift from Stimson’s home of geography and economics.
  • However, Stimson was able to actually apply his theories of analytical applied geography.
  • During this time Stimson moved to be the Chair of Urban Studies at Queensland University of Technology.

Disappointingly, I was not able to find much information on this project. Perhaps Ganis, Minnery & Mateo-Babiano (2014).

Later Career

1997 - Present

Queensland and Melbourne

  • In 1997 Stimson took on the role of Chair in Geographical Sciences and Planning at the University of Queensland.
  • He “began a substantial effort to develop a much more explicit focus on regional economic development and especially on measuring and modeling endogenous regional performance.”
  • Stimson was also director of and involved with a handful of groups at the University of Queensland and across Australia.
    • These groups researched homelessness, opportunity and vulnerability, and large-scale urban modeling.
  • In the 2010s Stimson was involved with developing online infrastructure to better enable spatial research with the University of Melbourne.

Conclusion

Summary

  • Although retired (as far as I can find) Stimson appears to still be writing and publishing. He is now about 81 years old.
  • Diverse set of experiences between teaching, research, university and organizational leadership, public policy, and regional planning.
  • Early career was more oriented toward competing for research dollars and developing survey methods.
  • Later career consisted of more leadership roles while researching public policy on a wide variety of subjects.
  • Greatly influenced by the quantitative revolution and prior quantitative thinkers.
  • Avidly opposed to the -isms of 1970s and 1980s.

Quotes

“It seems to me that a key factor is to be open to becoming engaged with researchers from other disciplinary backgrounds; to work in multi-disciplinary teams to bring to bear spatial analytic approaches to address issues of policy and business significance; and to be proactive in promulgating the potential contributions that can be made by geographers.”

“Only a firm commitment in human geography programs to an applied analytical paradigm can overcome the current malaise in the discipline and re-establish it as a science.”
